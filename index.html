What is No Fate?

No Fate is a deterministic boundary framework that distinguishes between questions AI systems can answer mechanically (deterministic outcomes) and questions requiring human judgment (interpretive or discretionary zones).

The framework establishes refusal as correctness: refusing to answer interpretive or discretionary questions is the normatively correct behavior, not a failure.

Core Principle: Humans, not machines, hold final authority over interpretive and discretionary legal questions.

Key Principles

  1. Boundaries, Not Outcomes - No Fate defines boundaries between deterministic and nondeterministic zones. It does not determine legal outcomes.
  2. Refusal as Correctness - Refusing interpretive or discretionary questions is normatively correct behavior, preserving human authority.
  3. Immutable Standards - Canonical documents are immutable and cryptographically verified. Updates use semantic versioning.
  4. Open Stewardship - Transparent governance with community participation and public accountability.

Canonical Documents

All canonical documents are immutable, cryptographically verified (SHA-256 + GPG), and versioned using semantic versioning.
